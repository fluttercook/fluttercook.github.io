Google's newest major release, **Android 17**, is rolling out to Pixel first and to other eligible devices through the rest of 2026. The theme this year is less about a single headline feature and more about three quiet shifts: better multitasking, deeper AI, and taking foldables seriously as a first-class form factor.

## Bubbles turn any app into a floating window

The most visible change for everyday users is **Bubbles** — the ability to turn any app into a floating window for quick multitasking. It is the kind of feature that sounds small and changes daily habits, letting you keep a chat, a calculator, or a translation floating above whatever you are doing. Android 17 also adds a new toolbar to record your screen and your reactions at the same time, a nod to the creator workflows that dominate mobile.

## Foldables get a gaming mode

Foldable phones have been on the market for years, but software has lagged the hardware. Android 17 introduces a **foldable gaming mode** that optimizes screen layout for the larger unfolded display. Combined with Google's broader hardware push — including the newly introduced Googlebook built with Gemini at its core — the message is that foldables and unconventional form factors are now part of the mainstream Android story, not an afterthought.

## Gemini Intelligence moves in

The strategic centerpiece is **Gemini Intelligence**, Google's on-device and cloud AI layer, arriving on select advanced devices "later this summer" and expanding across phones, watches, laptops, and cars. Google frames it as an assistant that "handles the busy work" so you can focus — the same proactive-assistant pitch Apple is making with Apple Intelligence, now fully joined on the Android side.

## Privacy and safety, tightened

Android 17 also ships meaningful privacy work: **temporary location access**, so an app can get your location once without permanent permission, and enhanced anti-theft features that can lock a stolen device. Google's monthly "Android Drop" updates reinforce this — recent drops added fake-call detection in the Phone app and extended Quick Share to work with AirDrop on more devices.

## What Android developers should do

- **Test on foldables and floating windows.** Bubbles and unfolded layouts expose apps that assume a single, fixed screen size. Responsive layout is no longer optional.
- **Respect temporary permissions.** Design flows that degrade gracefully when a user grants one-time location instead of always-on access.
- **Plan for an AI surface.** Whether or not you integrate Gemini directly, users increasingly expect assistant-style help inside apps.

For cross-platform teams, Flutter's responsive-layout tooling and its recent Android content-protection features make it well suited to the Android 17 world — a single codebase still has to earn its keep on a folding screen, but the primitives are there.

## The bottom line

Android 17 is an evolution, not a revolution — but the direction is clear. Multitasking is getting more fluid, AI is becoming ambient, and foldables are finally getting the software attention their hardware deserves. Developers who test across form factors and design for one-time permissions will be the ones whose apps feel native on Android in 2026.
